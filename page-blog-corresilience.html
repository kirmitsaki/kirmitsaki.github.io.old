Your personal performance coach on your phone

Overview

The Project

We were lucky to meet with the founders of what was to become CorResilience just as the seed of a powerful idea was being conceived whose ultimate aim was to drive personal and organisational resilience.

Together, through workshops and ongoing collaboration, we have taken this early stage concept and evolved it to what it is today: A dynamic new personal resilience application, enabling a company’s workforce to achieve profound & sustainable changes in their lifestyle habits, while actively reducing long term risk of disease.

My Role

I’m a part of the scrum team responsible for the strategy, design and delivery of the app for both Android and iOS.

I lead the design process, producing all major deliverables and presenting them to the client. Working closely with our PO and developers during our sprint-cycles, I get instant feedback and support to deliver the design stories in time.

The main challenge

The evolution of the CorPerformance offering to a more accessible digital platform that can be consumed by significantly more of a company’s workforce.

CorPerformance has dedicated over 10 years to developing coached behavioural change processes in the corporate market, with programmes all over Europe, the Far East and North America. The company began as a vehicle to distil the founders understanding of elite sport performance and resilience and deliver it into a corporate environment.

CorResilience will allow more of a company’s employees to feel the proven benefits of the original programme.

Our team’s main challenge was finding the best way to translate an analogue programme with a proven ROI into a robust digital offering of the same quality and value for the end user; a seamless UX experience that would keep them engaged and enable them to achieve sustainable change in their health and lifestyle.

The Approach

Defining requirements

Product Discovery

Through a structured interactive workshop, we took the client on the journey through the solution, defining the business model, the user interface and key functionality, summing all this information up in a clear product vision and story map.

The ultimate outcome of the workshop was to define the Minimum Viable Product, or MVP, ensuring that product features can be directly mapped back to the business goals to ensure that we are always addressing the key points we need to address.

Workshop Sections

The workshop was comprised of the following sections that all come together to bring the solution to life:

  • Populating the business model canvas
  • Building credible user personas
  • Creating and visualising proposed user journeys
  • Creating the functionality story map
  • Establishing the technical architecture

The Vision

Goals for the User

Enabling Resilience - Stress-related breakdown happens when demands placed on individuals exceed the available resources. The keys to effective and sustainable behavioural change, Recovery, Nutrition, Physical Activity and Wellness Mindset are the fundamental pillars the CorResilience app is based on.

Making Sustainable Changes in your Life - Using screening, education and coaching, the CorResilience application enables people to take control of their lifestyle and habits, understand what they need to change and why.

Goals for the Organisation

Leadership Development & Increase Productivity - Success drives success and individuals seeing the progress of their team and leaders around them is a massive driver of inspiration and commitment. The companies also observe Increased work efficiency, improved concentration levels and performance.

Stress Management - Lifestyle choices are a powerful tool to influence stress coping capacity.

Team Building - The process brings co-workers together on a common journey, outside the normal workplace boundaries, having a marked, positive impact on team building.

The MVP

The principles of MVP

“That version of a new product which allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort.”
- Eric Ries

The MVP has only the key features needed to satisfy early adopters and validate key assumptions of the product, enabling you to get it into the user’s hands as quickly as possible with the least development cost.

Working on the Build - Measure - Learn principle, the MVP is a version of the product that goes through the first loop of this cycle, requiring the minimum amount of effort and least amount of development time.

Ensuring user engagement and retention

Forrester Research 2015

US and UK smartphone owners use on average 24 apps, but spend more than 80 percent of their in-app time on just 5. Additionally, most users will abandon an app within 30 days of download.

Factors influencing early adoption and stickiness:

  • Solid onboarding experience: Designed to inspire and motivate the user
  • Gamification: Rewarding desired behaviours and renewed engagement
  • Community: Capitalising on social proof
  • Simplicity: Effortless ease of use
  • Strategic messaging: Careful alerts and notifications
  • Quality product performance: The app has to work well

Effectiveness

The CorResilience programme relies on fundamental assumptions that need to be true for the product to succeed:

  • Personalised health risk profile and journey has a motivating element to enable stickiness
  • Users will stick with the app once they have seen positive results
  • Goal setting and education is fundamental to sustained behaviour change
  • Users will engage with health education if it is available

Scrum Framework & UX Design

Design Toolkit

Axure & Overflow - used predominantly for crafting user flow diagrams and technical architecture diagrams.

Xtensio - used for creating user persona diagrams, really useful for PD documentation and templated documents.

Illustrator - proved to be the best tool of choice for prototyping and helped with the initial UI visualisation, iconography creation, creating prototype screens and high-level visuals that can be translated to high quality prints that were later uploaded on Proto.io to create the high-fidelity prototype.

The initial prototype made with Illustrator was not amended after it was delivered to our client. We decided to keep it as it was as a point of reference.

Sketch - When the development started I began using Sketch as I found it stronger in creating consistent and solid UI designs for the mobile app and communicate revisions with the scrum team. The prototype function was a powerful tool in creating transparency in our design process and strengthened our relationship with our client and allowed us to gain feedback and approval from the project’s stakeholders.

Zeplin - enabled the sharing of designs with the tech team. It saved my team a lot of time and keeps everything organized in one place. The developers can access the screens and see useful information on the dimensions, typography, colour palette and all the other components that they need to build fast the product.
